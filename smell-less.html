A Rose by any other name.... still won't smell like anything.

I've been alive 28 years, and without smell for potentially all of them. When I was young, I believed that smell was very much a learned ability, a skill. I thought that if I just sat down and reaally worked on smelling I could be as good as my friends, who could tell what we were having from lunch just sniffing the air briefly.

Gradually I figured that the must be something wrong with my sniffer. I went to the family doctor, and eventually got a catscan which provided us with no medical reasons why I couldnt smell. The best explanantion the specialist could give my mother and I was that I had had a horrible cold as a youngster that knocked out the nerve endings in my nose.

Living in a Smelly City, and Being "Nose-blind"

I've lived in Cedar Rapids for 24 years. Throughout those long, hard years I have heard millions of complaints from my peers and fellow Cedar Rapidians about the scent of the air in a variety of exotic locations throughtout the city.

Observing peoples reactions to external stimuli that I am completely oblivious to is pretty amusing. In this city, more often than not innocent citizens are clutching their noses and letting loose horrible barks of disgust. I calmly ask them, "What ever is the matter?" After we get past the fact that I am inexplicably impervious to the noxious smell particles, I often ask them to describe their emotions and their opinion on the origin of the particular smell.

All in all, being nose-blind and living in Cedar Rapids, Iowa is kind of a hoot. It would be kind of like being a duck living in Seattle, or a ball of fire living in Death Valley, never understanding the suffering around you.
